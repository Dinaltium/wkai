Support (Real-time AI)	Tutor CoPilot: A Human-AI Approach for Scaling Real-Time Expertise6	2024	NSSA Technical Report (Stanford)	Real-time tutor assistant that gives immediate “expert-like” suggestions to K–12 math tutors. An RCT (4,136 sessions) found a +4 percentage-point increase in student mastery (p<0.01), especially for less experienced tutors6.	Evaluated only with novice tutors in one district (mostly Hispanic students) over ~2 months7; no observed impact on year-end tests; interface was chat-only, not multimodal.

Smaller than top vision-language models; still lags behind GPT-4/Gemini on some tasks9. Specialized to UI/infographic domain, not tested on other screen environments.
Support (Multi-Agent Tutor)	MultiTutor: Collaborative LLM Agents for Multimodal Student Support10	2025	AI-Supported Education Workshop (PMLR)	A multi-agent tutoring framework where specialized LLM “agents” (content, visual, scaffold, etc.) collaborate. Uses web search and code generation to create multimodal content (images, animations) and provides tailored explanations, problems, and simulations10. Outperforms baseline LLMs on readability, depth, and diversity.	Prototype evaluation only: metrics-based comparison and case studies, no actual classroom trial. Complexity of coordinating many agents may affect scalability; real-world effectiveness is untested.
Support (Benchmark: Math)	MMTutorBench: The First Multimodal Benchmark for AI Math Tutoring11	2025	ArXiv	Introduces a benchmark of 685 math problems structured into three tutoring tasks (Insight, Formulation, Execution). Provides fine-grained rubrics. Evaluation of 12 multimodal LLMs reveals large performance gaps and highlights the challenge of OCR and reasoning in math tutoring11.	Benchmark-focused; covers only math domain and single-turn problems. Does not propose a system or multi-turn dialogue; limited to evaluation, not deployment.

Benchmark-only; uses short lecture clips and quiz questions, not an interactive teaching system. Focuses on offline understanding, not feedback or student modeling.

Shows large improvements (23-point win rate increase) by prompting the tutor with aggregated emotional context13. Limited evaluation (user study and metrics) without long-term educational outcomes.
Support (Programming Tutor)	LeafTutor: An AI Agent for Programming Assignment Tutoring14	2026	ArXiv	LLM-powered tutor for coding assignments that provides step-by-step guidance. Tested on real programming exercises; found guidance comparable to human TAs14, highlighting LLM potential in personalized STEM tutoring.	Early prototype with only a few Java assignments evaluated; tested by developers (not students). Depends on instructor-provided materials; real classroom validation is lacking.
